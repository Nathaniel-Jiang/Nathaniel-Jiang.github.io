Hi there — I’m Peng (Nathaniel) Jiang

PhD Student in Atmospheric Sciences at University of Washington

I’m a researcher with over four years of experience studying the formation and evolution of air pollutants (gases and aerosols) and their impact on air quality and human health. I hold a master’s degree in Power Engineering & Engineering Thermal Physics from Wuhan University of Technology (2022), where I worked under Prof. Yu Wang. Following that, I served as a research assistant at Hong Kong Polytechnic University under Dr. Ruoyu You. Now, I’m pursuing my PhD to deepen my expertise in aerosol science and air quality at the University of Washington.

RESEARCH

My research focuses on the formation and evolution of air pollutants, specifically gases and aerosols, and their impact on air quality and human health. I am interested in understanding how various pollutants form, their environmental implications, and potential strategies for mitigating their harmful effects on air quality.

PUBLICATIONS

Jiang, P., Xu, L., Wang, Q., Wang, Z., Chung, S. H., and Wang, Y.: Experimental and kinetic study on aromatic formation in counterflow diffusion flames of methane and methane/ethylene mixtures, Fuel, 354, 129304, 2023.

This paper discusses the experimental and kinetic study on aromatic formation in methane and methane/ethylene mixtures in counterflow diffusion flames.
