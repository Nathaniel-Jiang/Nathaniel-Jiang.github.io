Summary

With over four years of focused research experience, I have developed a deep interest in the formation and evolution of air pollutants (gases and aerosols) and their impact on air quality and human health. My drive to improve environmental conditions and enhance public health motivates me to continue expanding my knowledge and research skills. My current research interests include the formation and evolution of air pollutants, such as particulate matter, inorganic/organic aerosols, and volatile organic compounds, and their effects on air quality and public health. This research is pivotal for developing strategies to mitigate the adverse effects of air pollution on human health and the environment.

Project 5 (2022/08-2024/02)

Experimental study on mitigating the transmission of airborne pollutants in commercial aircraft using personalized ventilation and gasper system

Independently constructed and tested a full-scale, three-row Boeing 737 aircraft cabin facility, including a personalized displacement ventilation system and two manikins, to study the transmission of air pollutants in commercial airliners.

Project 4 (2021/08 - 2022/06)

Experimental and kinetic study on aromatic formation in counterflow diffusion flames of methane and methane/ethylene mixtures

Independently developed a method for measuring polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) using gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS) to qualify and quantify PAHs with up to three rings in counterflow diffusion flames (CDFs).

Project 3 (2021/05 - 2021/07)

Chemical and Sooting Structures of Counterflow Diffusion Flames of Butanol Isomers: An Experimental and Modeling Study

Conducted an experimental study of C1-C6 intermediates in butanol isomer flames using GC analysis to understand the effects of isomeric structures on sooting tendencies.

Project 2 (2021/01 - 2021/04)

Chemical speciation and soot measurements in laminar counterflow diffusion flames of ethylene and ammonia mixtures

Conducted an experimental study of C1-C6 intermediates in ethylene/ammonia flames using GC analysis to provide a comprehensive dataset on sooting characteristics and intermediate species for model validation, providing chemical kinetics insights into ammonia's effect on soot formation in CDFs.

Project 1 (2020/01 - 2020/12)

An experimental multiparameter investigation on the thermochemical structures of benchmark ethylene and propane counterflow diffusion flames and implications to their numerical modeling.

Independently developed a microprobe sampling system based on gas chromatography (GC) to achieve C1-C6 intermediate species measurement in counterflow diffusion flames (CDFs).
